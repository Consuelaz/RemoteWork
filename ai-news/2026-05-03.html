Model distillation involves using a powerful model as a teacher to guide a student model's outputs. The admission is particularly ironic given Musk's ongoing lawsuit against OpenAI, raising serious legal questions about IP boundaries in AI development.

ARC-AGI-3 Deep Dive: GPT-5.5 Scores 0.43%, Opus 4.7 Scores 0.18% — Three Core Failure Modes Identified

ARC Prize Foundation发布对GPT-5.5和Opus 4.7共160次ARC-AGI-3游戏运行的深度分析，揭示最新前沿模型的三大系统性推理缺陷。第一，模型能识别局部动作效果，却无法整合成完整世界模型——懂单步但不懂全局。第二，训练数据引发错误类比：GPT-5.5仅凭视觉相似性将未知环境误判为《打砖块》游戏，在错误假设上耗尽探索机会。第三，偶然通关不等于理解规则，错误理论被固化延续。GPT-5.5得分0.43%，每次完整测试耗费约1万美元；Opus 4.7得分0.18%。而人类无需任何先验知识即可达到100%。这份分析不只是打分，更是AI认知架构深层局限的X光片，AGI路上仍有巨大鸿沟。

The ARC Prize Foundation analyzed 160 game replays from GPT-5.5 and Opus 4.7, identifying three systematic reasoning failures in ARC-AGI-3 tasks. First: models track local effects but fail to build global world models. Second: training-induced false analogies lead models to misidentify novel environments as familiar games like Breakout. Third: accidental success locks in wrong theories. GPT-5.5 scored 0.43% at ~$10K per run; Opus 4.7 scored 0.18%. Humans score 100% with no prior knowledge, revealing a vast gap between current AI capabilities and genuine general intelligence.

Anthropic最新劳动力市场研究引入"观测暴露度"（Observed Exposure）新指标，区别于以往仅评估LLM理论能力的方法，结合真实雇佣变化数据进行测量。核心发现：当前AI对劳动力市场的冲击比舆论担忧的要温和；高暴露度职业（如数据录入、法务助理、初级内容创作）的就业增长预计在2034年前开始放缓。但需警惕：AI工具并不减少工作量，反而可能加剧工作强度、拉长工时。这份研究为当前"AI抢工作"恐慌提供了难得的实证基础——真正的变革正在发生，但节奏比预期更渐进，企业与从业者仍有窗口期建立结构化AI协作规范。

Anthropic's new labor market research introduces 'Observed Exposure,' a metric combining theoretical LLM capabilities with real hiring data. Key findings: AI's actual labor market impact remains more limited than public discourse suggests, with high-exposure jobs expected to see employment growth slowdown before 2034. However, AI tools appear to intensify work rather than reduce it. The study provides rare empirical grounding for AI labor displacement debates, suggesting a more gradual transformation than feared, though entry-level white-collar roles face clear structural headwinds.
