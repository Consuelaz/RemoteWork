Global RAM Shortage Driven by AI Data Centers Could Last Until 2030

AI 数据中心对 HBM（高带宽内存）的爆发式需求正在引发全球 RAM 短缺危机，且可能持续多年。SK 集团董事长表示短缺或延续至 2030 年；预计到 2027 年底，内存制造商仅能满足 60% 的需求。三大厂商（三星、SK 海力士、美光）正新建晶圆厂，但新产能最早 2027-2028 年才能上线。更关键的是，新工厂主要生产高利润的 AI 专用 HBM，导致消费级 DRAM 被严重挤压。三星 Galaxy 手机、微软 Surface 笔记本、Meta Quest 头显等消费产品已因此涨价。AI 算力繁荣的代价正在向整个科技产业链传导。

AI data centers' explosive demand for HBM is triggering a global RAM shortage that could persist for years. SK Group's chairman warns it may last until 2030, with memory manufacturers expected to meet only 60% of demand by end of 2027. The big three (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) are building new fabs, but capacity won't come online until 2027-2028. Crucially, new factories prioritize high-margin AI-focused HBM, severely squeezing consumer DRAM. Samsung Galaxy phones, Microsoft Surface laptops, and Meta Quest headsets have already seen price increases as AI compute prosperity's costs cascade through the tech supply chain.

Claude Opus 4.7 Released as a Deliberately Weakened Stepping Stone to Mythos

Anthropic 发布了 Claude Opus 4.7——目前最强大的「正式可用」模型，但这款产品的真正故事在于它被有意削弱了。Opus 4.7 在软件工程、图像分析和指令遵循上相比 4.6 有所提升，但在每一项评估中均低于本月早些时候发布的 Mythos Preview。Anthropic 明确表示 Opus 4.7「并未推进能力前沿」，其网络能力在训练中被差异化削弱。这是一个精心设计的安全策略：先在能力较低的模型上测试网络安全防护措施，验证有效后再将 Mythos 级别的能力广泛发布。Opus 4.7 定价与 4.6 一致（输入 $5/百万token，输出 $25/百万token），Cursor、Replit、Notion、Shopify 等公司已成为早期测试客户。

Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.7, its most powerful generally available model, but the real story is that it was deliberately weakened. While Opus 4.7 improves over 4.6 in software engineering, image analysis, and instruction following, it scores below Mythos Preview in every evaluation. Anthropic explicitly states Opus 4.7 'does not advance the capability frontier' — its cyber capabilities were differentially reduced during training. This is a carefully designed safety strategy: test cybersecurity safeguards on a less capable model first, then widely release Mythos-level capabilities once proven effective. Pricing matches 4.6 at $5/M input and $25/M output tokens, with Cursor, Replit, Notion, and Shopify among early testers.
